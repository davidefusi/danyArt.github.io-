Some of my Canvases

A depiction produced with chalk and charcoal to represent the black-and-white representation of living ordinary. Living in a small insignificant town with cloudy skies and white pavement; this is significant to the idea of a dull plain of living.
“Lighting Up the City”, a piece of work I created to represent the beauty a city can contain. Light and Color hold strength in a canvas, especially when being representative in such cities.
“The Oyster Bed”, is created with paint and sand to gather a thick composure of color and texture. This art piece is one of my best works as a painter, showing the beauty of South Carolinian marshes and wildlife.
“A Journey of Latin America,” detailed with a variety of Latin American monuments and iconic historical areas.
“Città viola”, created with acrylic paint of beautiful purple moonlight and bright white city lights. I got to improve my artistic eye on light with paint and my perception on acrylic composition in abstract art.
“Anxiety” was drawn in honor of “Inside Out 2.” I was so excited for the movie to come out; especially excited enough to draw the new internationally relatable character, Anxiety.
“Il Duomo di Milano” is inspired by the outstanding city of Milan. It holds value not only in the quality of detail in each representative line, but it holds the value of Italian history in the making.
“A Notre Dame Special”. This was created in admiration of gothic architecture. The Notre Dame is one of the most influential forms of historical construction, especially with its gothic architectural design.
“Defeated but Not Forgotten”. Inspired by a fantastically hilarious show “The Amazing Digital Circus,” this was created with immense detail to how the show holds plot and a whirlpool of emotions.
“Sicilian Charm”, in honor of my family’s incredible vacation to Sicily. The trip was so inspiring to me, especially since I love the power of Italian architectural history.
